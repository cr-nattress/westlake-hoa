## 4. HOA Board & Governance (High-Level)

**Board Authority**
- Adopts and amends policies, procedures, and rules
- Authorizes collections, legal actions, and referrals
- Retains legal counsel
- Oversees property management

**Board Actions**
- May act via meetings or unanimous written consent
- Must record votes for:
  - Collections referrals
  - Legal actions
  - Foreclosure authorization

## 7. Communications Log – HOA & Bold (High-Level Summary)

### Common Communication Patterns
- Bulk notices via AppFolio email
- Time-sensitive compliance and access requests
- Limited responsiveness to individual follow-ups
- Escalation to legal counsel for disputes or accommodations

### Notable Topics in Past Communications
- Pest control and extermination coordination
- Unit access demands
- Health & safety concerns
- Reimbursement questions
- Records requests
- Governance transparency issues

### Observed Issues
- Delayed or absent responses to owner emails
- Requirement to route requests through counsel
- Limited clarity on Board decisions and votes
- Inconsistent follow-up on health/safety matters

## 8. Records & Transparency

**Owners Are Entitled To**
- Meeting minutes
- Financial summaries
- Budgets
- Insurance summaries
- Policies and procedures
- Records inspection per CCIOA

**Restrictions**
- Attorney-client privileged materials excluded
- Executive session contents limited
- Personal owner data protected

**Observed Reality**
- Records access often delayed
- Formal written requests frequently required
- Board approval sometimes used as a gating mechanism

---

## 9. Enforcement & Compliance (Observed Behavior)

- Enforcement notices often originate from management
- Escalation path:
  Owner → Bold → Board → Legal Counsel
- Fines, access demands, and compliance notices rely on strict timelines
- Health/safety issues sometimes handled administratively rather than transparently
