Established in December 2017 by founding partners Daniel D. Reynolds and T.J. Corporate Genesis and Structural Evolution1.1 The Strategic Dissolution of Reynolds, Kalamaya & VoborilThe genesis of Alpenglow Law is rooted in a pivotal structural realignment within the Vail Valley’s legal market. Prior to late 2017, partners Daniel Reynolds and T.J. Voboril were key components of Reynolds, Kalamaya & Voboril, LLC (RKV Law). During their tenure, RKV Law experienced significant growth, more than doubling in size over a four-year period. While such expansion is often a marker of success, it introduces administrative complexities, overhead burdens, and a potential dilution of the personalized client-attorney relationship that is critical in boutique markets.In December 2017, the partners executed an "amicable dissolution" of RKV Law. This strategic divergence was not born of conflict but of a desire to refine operational focus. The dissolution allowed the partners to shed the weight of a larger organizational structure and "refocus efforts" on their respective specializations. For Reynolds and Voboril, this meant returning to a "back to basics" practice model that prioritized agility and direct client engagement over institutional scale. This move birthed Alpenglow Law, a firm designed to be leaner and more responsive to the fluctuations of the local resort economy.1.2 Geographic Positioning and Physical InfrastructureThe firm’s physical location is a calculated element of its accessibility strategy. Situated in the "Benchmark/Junction Building" in Avon, Colorado, the office places the firm at the nexus of the valley’s commercial activity. The building itself, housing popular local establishments like Loaded Joe's and Pazzo's Pizzeria, frames the law firm as an accessible, community-integrated entity rather than an aloof corporate fortress.However, the firm’s operational footprint extends beyond its physical walls. Recognizing the logistical challenges inherent in mountain travel—where weather and geography often impede movement—and the global nature of their client base (many of whom are non-resident property investors), Alpenglow Law has instituted robust virtual capabilities. The firm utilizes platforms such as Zoom and Google Meets to conduct conferences, ensuring that legal counsel is uninterrupted by physical distance or adverse weather conditions. This hybrid physical-digital presence is essential for servicing international clients involved in high-value Vail real estate transactions.2. Regulatory Environment and Credentialing FrameworkThe practice of law is a highly regulated profession, and the attorneys at Alpenglow Law must navigate a rigorous credentialing landscape to maintain their operational legitimacy. While the firm is based in Colorado, the standards they adhere to are reflective of broader national requirements for bar admission and ethical conduct.3.1 Bar Admission and CompetenceTo practice law in Colorado and represent clients in federal courts, Reynolds and Voboril have satisfied stringent educational and testing requirements.Educational Prerequisites: Both partners hold J.D. degrees from ABA-accredited law schools. This accreditation is a universal threshold for bar admission in U.S. jurisdictions, ensuring a standardized baseline of legal knowledge.Examination: Admission typically requires passage of the Uniform Bar Examination (UBE) or a state-specific equivalent at the time of their licensure, along with the Multistate Professional Responsibility Examination (MPRE). The MPRE tests an attorney’s knowledge of established ethical standards, a critical component for maintaining public trust.Reciprocity and Mobility: Voboril’s licensure history involves a transfer from Georgia to Colorado. This process, often governed by "Admission on Motion" rules based on years of practice, requires an attorney to demonstrate active, continuous practice (often 3 to 5 of the preceding 7 years) and good standing in their prior jurisdiction.3.2 Character, Fitness, and Ethical StandingBeyond academic and technical competence, the "Character and Fitness" inquiry is a cornerstone of legal licensure. Attorneys are vetted for "good moral character," which encompasses traits such as honesty, trustworthiness, and respect for the law.Financial Responsibility: The ability to manage client funds in trust accounts is predicated on a history of financial responsibility. Issues such as unpaid taxes or history of debt can be disqualifying, implying that the firm’s partners have maintained clean financial records.Ethical Compliance: The firm’s receipt of awards like the Martindale-Hubbell "Client Champion"  suggests not only technical proficiency but also adherence to high ethical standards in client communication and billing practices.4. Alpenglow Law provides definitive interpretations to guide Board actions and resolve internal conflicts.Modernization: The firm assists communities in amending their governing documents to reflect modern realities, such as the regulation of short-term rentals (VRBO/Airbnb) and the implications of legalized marijuana on community nuisances.6.2 Enforcement and ComplianceA significant portion of HOA practice involves enforcement.Covenant Enforcement: The firm represents HOAs in enforcing aesthetic and behavioral standards (e.g., noise, architectural compliance). Alpenglow Law manages the collection process, which can escalate to the foreclosure of assessment liens—a powerful statutory remedy in Colorado that allows an HOA to foreclose on a unit for unpaid dues.7.
