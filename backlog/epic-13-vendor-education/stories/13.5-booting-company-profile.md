### 2. Important: Your Rights When Booted
**This section should be prominently displayed near the top of the page.**

#### Fee Limits (Under Colorado Law)
| Fee Type | Maximum |
|----------|---------|
| Standard boot removal | $160 |
| Payment to release boot (HB25-1117) | $60 |
| Dual boot removal | Check regulations |

#### Time Limits for Release
| Situation | Maximum Wait Time |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Business hours | 90 minutes |
| After hours | 120 minutes |

#### You CANNOT Be Booted For:
- Expired registration alone
- Being inside your vehicle (occupied vehicle)
- Without proper signage (1 sq ft minimum at entrance)
- Without 24-hour written notice (with some exceptions under HB25-1117)

#### Your Rights Include:
- Right to documented boot removal receipt with unique serial number
- Right to complaint contact information on boot
- Right to file PUC complaint
- Right to pay partial amount ($60 max) for release under 2025 law

### 3. If You Believe You Were Wrongfully Booted

**Step-by-Step Guide:**

1. **Document Everything**
   - Take photos of your vehicle, the boot, signage (or lack thereof)
   - Note the exact time you discovered the boot
   - Record your location in the parking lot
   - Save any parking passes, receipts, or proof of permission

2. **Check for Valid Parking Authorization**
   - Did you have a valid guest pass?
   - Were you in a designated visitor spot?
   - Did you have permission from a resident?

3. **Request Release**
   - Call (970) 306-8687 ext. 1 for boot removal
   - Under HB25-1117, you may request release by paying no more than $60 upfront
   - Note the time you called and arrival time of technician

4. **File a Complaint**
   - **Colorado PUC:** (303) 894-2070 (option #2) or toll-free (800) 456-0858
   - **Town of Avon Police:** Contact Sgt. Matt Jamison
   - **BBB Complaint:** bbb.org

5. **Know the Limitations**
   - Company's terms state all payments are **non-refundable**
   - Disputes require **binding arbitration** per their terms
   - Collections add **50% additional fee**

### 6. Colorado Regulatory Framework

#### Governing Authority
- **Colorado Public Utilities Commission (PUC)**
- Regulations: 4 CCR 723-6 (Rules 6800-6899)

#### Key Requirements for Booting Companies
- Must obtain PUC permit ($150-153 fee)
- Criminal background checks required
- Minimum insurance requirements
- Must disclose principal owners
- PUC can suspend/revoke permits for violations

#### Recent Legislation: HB25-1117 (2025)
Signed by Governor Jared Polis, this law:
- Requires 24-hour written notice before immobilization (with exceptions)
- Prohibits indiscriminate patrolling
- Mandates $60 maximum payment option for boot release
- Requires individual permission from property owners within 24 hours
